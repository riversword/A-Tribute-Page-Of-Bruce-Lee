Bruce Lee

An actor, philosopher, film director, producer, screenwriter and martial artist.

Lee Jun-fan (Chinese: 李振藩; November 27, 1940 – July 20, 1973), known professionally as Bruce Lee, was a Hong Kong and American actor, film director, martial artist, martial arts instructor, philosopher and founder of the martial art Jeet Kune Do. Lee was the son of Cantonese opera star Lee Hoi-chuen. He is widely considered by commentators, critics, media, and other martial artists to be one of the most influential martial artists of all time, and a pop culture icon of the 20th century. He is often credited with helping to change the way Asians were presented in American films.

TimeLine

  • 1940- In the The Year of the Dragon, Lee Jun Fan, Bruce Lee is born in San Francisco Chinatown. At three months old, Bruce plays role of a female baby, carried by his father.
  • 1941- Bruce's family return to Hong Kong.
  • 1946- Bruce makes his first major childhood movie in The Beginning of a Boy. Later this year, Bruce appears in 20 more films in Asia.
  • 1953-Hong Kong- Bruce begins to train under Sifu Yip Man, a master of the Wing Chun system of Kung-Fu.
  • 1958-Hong Kong- Bruce enters the 1958 Boxing Championships and defeats the reigning three year champion, Gary Elms.
  • 1959-Hong Kong- Because of numerous street fighting, Bruce's family decide that Bruce should take a three week voyage to the United States.
  • 1961 March-Seattle- Bruce enrolls at the University of Washington, studying Philosophy. He teaches Kung-Fu to students at school.
  • 1963 October 25-Seattle- Bruce starts the first Jun Fan Kung-Fu Institute. He teaches any person of any race.
  • 1964 August 2-Long Beach- Bruce shows off his "one-inch punch," and his two-finger push-ups.
  • 1965-Oakland- Bruce is bothered on why the fight took so long and begins to re-evaluate his style. Then, the early concepts of Jeet Kune Do (JKD), "The art of the intercepting fist" is created. JKD is an art including techniques of all types of fighting. (i.e. American Boxing, Thai Kick Boxing, Japanese Karate, etc.) His style is no style.
  • 1966-Los Angeles- Bruce begins working on a new TV series called The Green Hornet as Kato.
  • 1967-Hollywood- He meets Chuck Norris in New York at the All American Karate Championships in Washington D.C. Chuck fights Joe Lewis and wins.
  • 1970-Los Angeles- Bruce injures his back while training. During the months of recovery he starts to document his training methods and his philosophy of Jeet Kune Do.
  • 1971 July-Thailand- Filming begins for The Big Boss (released in the U.S. as Fists of Fury). The Big Boss opens in Hong Kong proceeds to gross more than $3.5 million in little than three weeks.
  • 1972 Hong Kong- Bruce begins work on Game of Death and films several fight scenes including Danny Inosanto and Kareem Abdul-Jabbar.
  • 1972-Rome Italy- Location shots are made for Bruce's third film The Way of the Dragon (released in the U.S. as The Return of the Dragon). This time Bruce gets almost complete control the the movie, which he writes, directs, and stars in. Chuck Norris is Bruce's adversary in the final fight scene. Again, this film surpasses all records set by his previous two films.
  • 1973 April-Hong Kong- Filming of Enter the Dragon is completed.
  • 1973 July 20-Hong Kong- Bruce Lee dies in Hong Kong of an apparent cerebral edema (swelling of the brain).
  • 1973 July 25-Hong Kong- A funeral ceremony is held for friends and fans in Hong Kong consisting of over 25,000 people. Bruce is dressed in the Chinese outfit he wore in Enter the Dragon.
